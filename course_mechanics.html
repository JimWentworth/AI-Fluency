Start Here: Technology, Privacy, & Expectations

What to do first: Use the tabs below to understand what you need for this course, how privacy works, what’s expected of you, and where to go for help. You don’t need to memorize this—just know where it is.

Technology Overview & Requirements

This program uses Canvas plus optional external generative AI tools to support learning, experimentation, and reflection. Tools may change over time; learning goals do not.

Required technology

  • Canvas access
  • Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
  • Reliable internet connection
  • Ability to read and create text-based documents

Recommended login strategy

Many tools support “Sign in with Google.” Using a Google account can reduce friction, simplify access, and improve cross-tool workflows.

  • Fewer passwords and account recovery issues
  • Smoother access across tools
  • Easier document management
  • More consistent experience across devices

Recommended, not required.

Accessibility note: If a tool presents barriers, you may use an alternative. The goal is demonstrating understanding—not using a specific interface.

Privacy & Data Use

CITL minimizes data collection and does not monitor activity inside AI tools.

CITL may collect

  • Enrollment and completion status
  • Required submissions you explicitly provide
  • Optional feedback
  • Aggregated participation metrics

CITL does not collect

  • AI prompts or chat histories
  • Uploaded documents unless submitted
  • Personal account data
  • Job or teaching performance data

Rule of thumb: If you wouldn’t post it publicly, don’t upload it to an AI tool unless it’s explicitly approved for sensitive data.

Communication Expectations

This course values judgment and reflection over correctness or compliance.

You can expect

  • Clear instructions
  • Transparent expectations
  • Respect for autonomy
  • Feedback focused on learning

You are expected to

  • Engage thoughtfully
  • Evaluate AI outputs critically
  • Use AI as support, not authority
  • Explain how AI was used when relevant

Where to Go for Help

CITL supports learning and course questions. You are responsible for managing third-party tool accounts.

Fastest fix: Switch tools or browsers if something isn’t working. Flexibility is built into the course.

Responsible Use & Academic Integrity

AI generates plausible text, not verified truth. You remain responsible for outcomes.

  • Verify facts
  • Revise drafts
  • Disclose meaningful AI use
  • Own decisions
