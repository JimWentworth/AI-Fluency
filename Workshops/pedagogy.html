Assignment Design Lab

Goal: Shift from "What do students produce?" to "How do students learn?"

UIUC CITL recommends "Authentic Assessment" strategies that prioritize human-in-the-loop critical thinking over rote content generation.

AI-RESISTANT

Scaffolded Process Portfolios

Break large projects into non-generative milestones. Grade the evolution of thought rather than the final draft.

  • Step 1: Handwritten concept map of research questions.
  • Step 2: Annotated bibliography with "Personal Connection" rationale for each source.
  • Step 3: In-class peer review workshop using tracked changes.
AI-INTEGRATED

The AI-Critique Model

Students generate an initial response using an AI tool, then spend the remainder of the assignment "acting as the expert" to refine it.

  • Identify 3 factual "hallucinations" or logical gaps in the AI output.
  • Rewrite the section using specific course readings (not in AI training data).
  • Submit a "Response to Reviewer" memo detailing all corrections made.
AI-RESISTANT

Hyper-Local Case Studies

Require students to apply course theories to campus-specific or current-week events at the University of Illinois.

Example: "Apply urban planning principles to the proposed 2026 redesign of Green Street."

Design Decision Tree

Ask these three questions before finalizing your assignment:

  1. Is the "struggle" the point? If yes, use proctored, in-person writing.
  2. Is this a professional skill? If yes, teach them how to use AI as a tool, not a replacement.
  3. Can it be localized? Require references to specific class discussions or local data.
