Conceptual Grounding: What Customization Is and Isn’t

The Three Layers of Customization

1. Prompt-Level

One-off instructions inside a single conversation. You shape behavior moment by moment: roles, constraints, output formats, and examples.

2. Persistent Personalization

Settings that carry across conversations: memory, custom instructions, tone and style preferences, and reusable prompt frameworks.

3. Structural Customization

Custom GPTs, uploaded datasets, or agent-like workflows designed for recurring tasks, such as syllabus review or assignment feedback support.

Why Customization Exists

  • Reduce repetitive explanation and setup.
  • Increase consistency in tone, structure, and criteria.
  • Align outputs with disciplinary and institutional norms.
  • Make complex workflows easier to repeat and adapt.
Key idea: Customization does not make the model “smarter.” It narrows and directs its behavior so you get more predictable results for specific kinds of work.

Quick Check: What Are You Trying to Improve?

Before you build anything, identify the main pain point you’re addressing:

  • Time: Are you tired of rewriting the same instructions?
  • Quality: Do outputs vary too much in tone or structure?
  • Alignment: Do you need outputs to match a rubric, policy, or style guide?
