Integrity, Ethics & Privacy

The Truth About AI Detectors

UIUC CITL does not currently recommend relying on AI detection software for disciplinary action. These tools have high false-positive rates, particularly for students who are non-native English speakers or neurodivergent. Focus on process-based evidence instead.

The "Inquiry-Based" Conversation

If a student's work is suspiciously inconsistent with their previous performance, use this non-confrontational script to discuss their process.

Step 1: The Opening "I noticed your writing style in this essay is quite different from your in-class work. Can you talk me through how you developed your main argument?"
Step 2: Exploring Sources "This paragraph cites a specific theory we haven't covered yet. Could you explain your understanding of this concept and how you found this source?"
Step 3: Addressing AI Directly "In this course, we value your unique voice. Did you use any digital tools to help draft or refine this? I'd like to understand your workflow."

Privacy (FERPA)

Never upload student work or identifiable data into public AI models. At Illinois, only use University-approved enterprise instances for handling sensitive information.

Algorithmic Bias

Remind students that AI training data often reflects societal biases. Critical evaluation of AI output for gender, racial, and cultural prejudice is a required skill.
