The C.P.T. Prompting Framework

For high-quality results, always include three components in your instructions:

  • Context: Course level, student background, and learning goals.
  • Persona: Who should the AI act as? (e.g., Expert Historian, Socratic Tutor).
  • Task: The specific format and content you need.
Pro-Tip: Use the Boldly IT GenAI Portal to access enterprise-grade models that protect your data and intellectual property.

1. Rubric Generator

Generate a draft rubric based on your assignment description.

COPY & PASTE "Act as an expert instructional designer at the University of Illinois. Create a 4-point analytic rubric for a [Assignment Type] about [Topic]. Include criteria for critical thinking, source integration, and clarity. Format as a table."

2. Reading Level Level-Set

Ensure your assignment instructions are accessible to all students.

COPY & PASTE "Analyze the following assignment instructions. Identify jargon that might confuse a first-year undergraduate. Suggest three ways to simplify the language while maintaining academic rigor."

3. Low-Stakes Quiz Creator

Turn lecture notes into study aids instantly.

COPY & PASTE "Using the attached lecture notes, generate 5 multiple-choice questions and 2 short-answer reflection questions. For each question, provide a brief explanation of why the correct answer is right."
