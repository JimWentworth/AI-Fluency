Prompting Basics

Fundamental principles of effective prompt engineering

Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting effective inputs to guide AI models toward desired outputs. Clear, specific prompts lead to better results.

Core Principles

1. Be Clear and Specific

Avoid ambiguity. The more specific your instructions, the better the results.

Vague:

"Tell me about Python"

Problem: Could refer to the programming language, the snake, or Monty Python.

Clear:

"Explain the key differences between Python lists and tuples in programming, with code examples"

2. Provide Context

Give the AI relevant background information to frame responses appropriately.

With Context:

"I'm a beginner learning web development. Can you explain what APIs are using a simple analogy, then give one practical example?"

3. Specify the Format

Tell the AI how you want the information structured.

Format Specified:

"List the top 5 benefits of regular exercise. For each benefit, provide a 1-sentence explanation."

Advanced Techniques

Chain-of-Thought Prompting: Ask the AI to "think step by step" for complex problems.
Chain-of-Thought:

"A store has a 25% off sale, and you have an additional 10% coupon. If an item costs $80, what's the final price? Show your work step by step."

Key Takeaway: Great prompts are clear, specific, contextual, and well-structured.
