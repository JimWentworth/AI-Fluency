Ethics, Privacy & Risk in Customization

Key Questions Before You Customize

  • What data are you asking the system to store or reuse?
  • Could any of that data identify a specific student or colleague?
  • Would you be comfortable explaining your setup to a student, chair, or IRB?
  • Are you accidentally assigning “expert authority” to an AI configuration?

Scenarios for Discussion

Scenario 1: Grading Assistant GPT

A faculty member builds a custom GPT, uploads a full set of student essays, and asks it to generate rubric-aligned feedback and suggested grades.

  • What are the potential benefits?
  • What are the most serious risks?
  • How might this be redesigned to reduce risk while still saving time?

Scenario 2: Policy Explainer GPT

A department shares a GPT trained on public-facing policy documents (e.g., syllabi, student handbooks) to help students navigate key procedures.

  • Where could the model mislead students?
  • How should the department disclose limitations?
  • What maintenance will be needed over time?

Safer Practices

  • Avoid uploading identifiable student work into external tools.
  • Prefer institutionally supported, secure systems for sensitive data.
  • Clearly disclose when AI-assisted tools are used in a course.
  • Document your customization choices and keep a simple change log.
