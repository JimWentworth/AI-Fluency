Which AI Tool Should I Use?

A quick decision guide for students, staff, and faculty exploring general, research, productivity, and media-focused AI tools in an Illinois-aligned interface.

Flipcards: Match Your Task to the Right Tool

Hover or tap each card to flip. The front describes your task; the back suggests AI tools that fit that goal. This is not an endorsement list, but a starting point for informed choice.

General

I need help writing, brainstorming, or analyzing.

Draft text, explore ideas, or get help reasoning through problems and options.

Try These

  • ChatGPT — all-purpose writing and reasoning.
  • Claude — strong for long or complex documents.
  • Gemini — seamless inside Google Docs and other apps.
  • Napkin AI — organizes messy ideas into visual structure.
  • Notion AI — ideal if your work lives in Notion.
Choose one as your main workspace; switch tools only when the task truly requires it.
Research

I need evidence, citations, or document-based answers.

Ground your work in sources, summaries, and analysis of real documents or the web.

Try These

  • Perplexity — answers with citations and links.
  • NotebookLM — summarizes and synthesizes your uploaded files.
  • Gemini — good for multimodal and web-grounded queries.
Never rely on a single summary; open a few sources yourself and verify.
Productivity

I use Word, PowerPoint, Excel, or Outlook.

You want AI directly inside Microsoft 365 to draft, summarize, and transform content.

Try These

  • Microsoft Copilot — integrated into M365 apps.
  • ChatGPT — useful for drafting before paste-back.
Works best when your documents are stored in OneDrive or SharePoint.
Secure

I’m working with sensitive or internal university data.

Use institutionally approved tools for anything involving student or private information.

Try These

  • Illinois Chat — campus-secure and FERPA-aligned.
  • Copilot for M365 — strong enterprise protections when configured by IT.
When in doubt, treat non-public information as requiring a campus-approved system.
Images

I need an illustration, diagram, or concept sketch.

Generate visuals for slides, handouts, prototypes, or conceptual exploration.

Try These

  • Adobe Firefly — safer licensing and strong academic visuals.
  • Leonardo — custom styles, concept art, and rich illustration.
  • Midjourney — highly aesthetic imagery (when available).
  • Napkin AI — conceptual diagrams and structured maps.
Verify all scientific, technical, or historical details before teaching with AI images.
Avatar Video

I want a talking-head or presenter-style video.

Create short explainer videos with a virtual presenter or avatar.

Try These

  • HeyGen — straightforward avatar videos from a script.
  • Synthesia — polished training and corporate-style videos.
  • D-ID — quick talking-photo clips.
Do not generate the likeness of real people without explicit, informed consent.
Creative Video

I want short, creative, or experimental clips.

Use generative video for visual moods, storyboards, or abstract motion.

Try These

  • Runway Gen-2 — versatile generative video creation.
  • Pika Labs — playful, experimental short-form clips.
Treat these as idea prototypes, not final production-quality content.
Voice

I need narration, voiceover, or synthetic speech.

Create audio for slides, video, podcasts, or accessibility support.

Try These

  • ElevenLabs — high-quality, natural-sounding voices.
  • Descript — narration plus simple editing and Overdub voice options.
Only clone voices with explicit permission and appropriate disclosure.
Notes

I need to organize notes, ideas, or tasks.

Use AI to help structure, connect, and refine your knowledge and plans.

Try These

  • Notion AI — integrates notes, docs, and tasks in one place.
  • Napkin AI — maps ideas into connected concepts and flows.
  • Claude / ChatGPT — rewriting, summarizing, and reorganizing notes.
Let AI help with structure; keep human judgment on meaning and priority.
Unsure

I’m not sure what tool I need yet.

Start with flexible, general-purpose options and adjust as your task becomes clearer.

Start Here

  • ChatGPT — best general starting point for most tasks.
  • Perplexity — best if you care most about research and citations.
  • Adobe Firefly — simple, safe visuals if you need images.
  • HeyGen — easy entry for short avatar-style videos.
Learn one tool’s strengths and limits, then branch out selectively.

Tip: On desktop, hover to flip cards. On touch devices, tap. With a keyboard, tab to a card and press Enter or Space to read both sides.
