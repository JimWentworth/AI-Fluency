Customization Map

Choosing the Right Level of Customization

Not every task needs a custom agent, and not every workflow should live in a one-off prompt. Use this visual guide to decide when to stay simple and when to invest in deeper structure.

Level 1

Prompt-Level Customization

Situational · Ephemeral · Safest starting point

  • Shape one conversation at a time.
  • Specify role, task, audience, format, and constraints.
  • Ideal for experiments, quick drafts, and one-off questions.
Use when the task is unique or exploratory.
Level 2

Persistent Personalization

Custom Instructions · Memory · Defaults

  • Store stable preferences about tone, format, and reasoning style.
  • Shape how the AI responds by default across conversations.
  • Best for recurring patterns you want every answer to follow.
Use when repetition is costly.
Level 3

Structural Customization

Agents · RAG · Workflows · Projects

  • Build scoped tools around specific documents or tasks.
  • Use retrieval and rules for consistent, grounded behavior.
  • Shared, reusable, and higher stakes than personal prompts.
Use when others will rely on the system.

Decision Guide: Which Level Do I Need?

Read down the questions and glance right at the recommended tier.
Question 1
Is this a one-off or highly varied task?
Example: rewriting a single email, clarifying a concept, exploring an idea.
Question 2
Will I repeat this pattern frequently, with similar goals and expectations?
Example: weekly reading summaries, standard feedback structure, preferred tone.
Question 3
Do other people need to rely on this and trust it to use specific sources or rules?
Example: policy Q&A, program help-bots, shared tutoring tools.
Rule of thumb
Start as low as possible on the ladder (prompt-level) and move up only when repetition or shared use makes the investment worthwhile.
Level 1
If the task is unique or exploratory
Stay at prompt-level customization. Design a clear, structured prompt for this situation and keep your changes local to this conversation. No persistent settings needed.
Level 2
If the pattern repeats for you personally
Promote the pattern into persistent instructions. Encode stable preferences in Custom Instructions or memory so you don’t have to restate them every time.
Level 3
If others will depend on it, or it needs documents
Invest in structural customization: a project, RAG-backed agent, or custom GPT with clear scope, curated sources, and documented limitations. Treat it like shared infrastructure.
