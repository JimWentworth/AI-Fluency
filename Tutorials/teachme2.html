Prompts & Temperature: The Foundation

15 min Core Concepts ChatGPT Copilot
‚ñº

Core Concepts

Prompt: The input text or instructions given to an AI model to guide its response or behavior.

Temperature: A parameter that controls randomness in text generation. Lower values (0.0-0.3) make output focused and deterministic; higher values (0.7-1.0) increase creativity and variability.

ChatGPT Microsoft Copilot

Exercise: Test Temperature Settings

  1. Open ChatGPT (accessible via campus license)
    Explain quantum entanglement to an undergraduate physics student in 3 sentences.
  2. Generate the response 3 times by clicking regenerate. Notice how the core information stays similar but the phrasing varies.
  3. Now try a more creative prompt:
    Write a creative opening line for a research paper about climate change impacts on migration patterns.
  4. Regenerate this 3 times. Notice the increased variability in creative tasks.

ü§î Reflection Questions

  • When would you want more deterministic (lower temperature) outputs in your work?
  • When might higher variability be beneficial?
  • How does this relate to grading student work that may use AI?

‚úì Key Takeaways

  • Clear, specific prompts yield better results than vague requests
  • AI outputs vary even with identical prompts due to temperature settings
  • You can't always recreate exact AI outputs‚Äîthis matters for academic integrity discussions

Tokens & Context Windows: Understanding Limits

12 min Core Concepts Claude ChatGPT
‚ñº

Core Concepts

Tokens: Basic units of text that AI models process, roughly equivalent to words or word fragments. A typical word is 1-2 tokens.

Context Window: The maximum amount of text (in tokens) that an AI model can consider at once, including both input and output.

Claude ChatGPT

Exercise: Test Context Limits

  1. Upload a research paper or long document (5-10 pages) to Claude
    Summarize the main argument and key findings of this paper in 3 paragraphs.
  2. Follow up with questions that require the full context:
    How does the methodology in section 3 support the conclusions in section 5?
  3. Try the same with an even longer document (20+ pages) and notice if the AI asks you to specify which sections to focus on.

ChatGPT (128K tokens)

~96,000 words or ~300 pages

Good for: Most research papers, book chapters

Claude (200K tokens)

~150,000 words or ~500 pages

Good for: Entire books, dissertations, large datasets

Important: When working with very long documents, AI may lose track of earlier details. Break complex analyses into focused questions.

‚úì Key Takeaways

  • Different AI tools have different context window limits‚Äîchoose accordingly
  • Longer context doesn't always mean better understanding of all details
  • For comprehensive analysis, chunk your work into focused queries

Identifying Hallucinations & Using RAG

20 min Core Concepts Perplexity NotebookLM
‚ñº

Core Concepts

Hallucinations: When AI models generate false or nonsensical information that appears plausible, often occurring when the model lacks sufficient knowledge or context.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): A technique that enhances AI responses by retrieving relevant information from external sources before generating answers, improving accuracy and reducing hallucinations.

Perplexity ChatGPT NotebookLM

Exercise: Compare Outputs & Verify Claims

  1. Ask ChatGPT for specific information from your field:
    What were the key findings of the [specific recent study] published in [specific journal] in 2024?

    Note: Pick a real but somewhat obscure recent study from your field.

  2. Now ask the same question in Perplexity (which uses RAG by default)

    Perplexity will search the web and cite sources for its response.

  3. Compare the outputs:
    • Did ChatGPT provide citations? Were they accurate?
    • Did Perplexity find the actual study and cite it correctly?
    • Which response is more trustworthy for research purposes?
  4. Advanced: Use NotebookLM with your own documents

    Upload 3-5 papers from your research area and ask questions. NotebookLM only answers based on uploaded documents, eliminating hallucinations.

Without RAG (ChatGPT)

Pros: Fast, conversational, creative

Cons: May hallucinate facts, no citations, training data cutoff

Best for: Brainstorming, writing assistance, general questions

With RAG (Perplexity, NotebookLM)

Pros: Cites sources, current information, verifiable

Cons: May miss nuanced interpretations

Best for: Research, fact-checking, literature review

ü§î Critical Questions

  • When did you notice hallucinations? What made them seem plausible?
  • How would you teach students to identify potential hallucinations?
  • What verification steps should be non-negotiable in academic work?

‚úì Key Takeaways

  • All AI models can hallucinate‚Äîespecially with specific, recent, or niche information
  • RAG-based tools (Perplexity, NotebookLM) significantly reduce hallucinations by grounding responses in real sources
  • Always verify critical claims, especially for research and publication
  • Choose tools based on whether you need creative generation or factual accuracy
