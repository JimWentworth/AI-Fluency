Design Process, Transparency, and Provenance

Internal working norms (institutional visibility)

Why this exists

The certificate is built using iterative design work that may include AI-assisted drafting. To keep the work defensible, reusable, and collaboratively maintainable, we treat process transparency as a core design norm.

What “behind-the-scenes documentation” means (internal)

We preserve enough context for future collaborators (and future-us) to answer:

  • What was the intent?
  • What constraints were we designing under?
  • What tradeoffs were considered?
  • What decision did we make, and why?
  • What should not be inferred (boundaries / non-goals)?

This is not a demand for polished memos. Draft notes are acceptable. Legibility beats perfection.

AI use in certificate creation (norms)

AI may assist with: synthesis, drafting, reframing, checklists, template generation, and formatting support. AI should not: silently erase debate, remove uncertainty, or “optimize” away intentional boundaries.

When AI is used, capture:

  • purpose of the AI use (brainstorm, rewrite, structure, etc.)
  • key constraints given to the AI
  • what the human accepted, modified, or rejected
  • anything that remains uncertain

Provenance artifacts we maintain (recommended set)

  • Canonical overview (stable “source of truth”)
  • Design rationale notes (decisions + why)
  • Canvas/DesignPLUS implementation conventions
  • Branding/accessibility guardrails
  • Tool documentation references (official docs links)

Non-goals (to prevent drift)

  • Platform certification or vendor training
  • “Best prompts” mastery as the primary outcome
  • ML engineering or coding specialization (unless elective)
  • Treating AI as an authority in academic decisions
