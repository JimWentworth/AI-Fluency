Tool Highlight: HeyGen (Free Browser Version)

AI video avatars and lip-synced narration for short announcements, intros, and demos.

In this walkthrough, you’ll go from creating a free HeyGen account to generating a short avatar-led video—while keeping an eye on free-tier limits like time caps, watermarks, and generation credits.

What HeyGen is (and the free-tier reality)

HeyGen creates AI-generated videos where an avatar (stock or custom) speaks your script with realistic lip-syncing and gestures. It’s widely used for course intros, announcements, micro-lessons, translation/dubbing, and onboarding videos.

On the free plan, you typically get:

  • Limited monthly video-generation credits
  • Short video length per generation (usually ~1 minute)
  • Watermarked videos
  • Restricted access to high-end avatars or custom avatar creation
  • Limited lip-sync quality compared to paid tiers
Campus lens

Treat the free tier as a prototype sandbox—a way to see what avatar-based video feels like. For polished or repeated course content, most instructors eventually use higher tiers or alternative workflows.

Create or log in to a free HeyGen account
  1. Go to https://www.heygen.com.
  2. Click Sign Up or Login.
  3. Sign up using:
    • Email + password
    • Google, Apple, or another supported SSO option

Free-tier caution

The free plan may show ads for upgrades and restrict access to certain avatars or styles. That’s normal—just choose any avatar without a “Pro” or “Premium” tag.

2
Tour the HeyGen browser interface

The HeyGen interface is built around a card-style dashboard and a drag-and-drop editor:

  • Dashboard: Choose templates, start new videos, explore avatars, or access the Talking Photo feature.
  • Editor canvas: Where you place your avatar, background, and text overlay.
  • Avatar panel: A catalog of stock avatars (some free, some paid).
  • Script panel: Where you paste or write the text the avatar will read.
  • Settings panel: Voice, language, pacing, video ratio, subtitles, and background options.
Mental model

If ElevenLabs = “AI audio,” and Firefly = “AI images,” then HeyGen = “AI presenter for tiny videos.” It shines when the video is short and highly scripted.

3
Create a simple avatar video (free-tier workflow)
  1. From the dashboard, click Create Video.
  2. Select a Template or start with a blank canvas.
  3. Choose an avatar:
    • Pick one without a “Pro” tag
    • Consider diversity, tone, and formality depending on your audience
  4. Paste your script in the Script box. Aim for:
    • 100–130 words (≈ 30–45 seconds)
    • Short sentences
    • Clear pronunciation cues for names or jargon
  5. Choose voice + language. Free plans include a subset of voice options.
  6. Adjust settings:
    • Aspect ratio – 16:9 for slides, 9:16 for mobile
    • Background – solid color, gradient, image upload
    • Subtitles – recommended for accessibility
  7. Click Generate. HeyGen processes your video (usually 30 seconds–2 minutes).
  8. Review the result. Free-tier exports typically include a watermark and may limit quality.
Activity: One concept, two styles

Pick a 60–90 word script explaining a core idea from this certificate. Generate:

  • Version A: Formal avatar + neutral background
  • Version B: More casual avatar + colorful background

Compare: How does tone shift based on avatar choice and background? Which is more effective for your actual learners?

Free-tier limits to watch carefully

HeyGen’s free tier is generous enough to experiment, but it has several constraints you’ll hit if you’re not careful:

  • Monthly video-generation credits: Each generation consumes credits. Long or multiple takes will drain your monthly allowance.
  • Time limits: Free videos are usually capped around 30–60 seconds. Plan your script accordingly.
  • Watermarks: Free exports include a HeyGen watermark.
  • No advanced avatars: Many of the most realistic or expressive avatars require a paid plan.
  • No custom avatar creation: Uploading your own likeness (photo/video) typically requires paid tiers and explicit consent steps.
  • Lip-sync quality variance: Shorter, clearer scripts look best.
Tips for staying inside limits
  • Keep scripts short and focused—30–45 seconds is ideal.
  • Fix your script before generating—don’t rely on multiple drafts.
  • Reuse backgrounds and templates to avoid re-rendering.
  • Save your generated clips locally before starting new ones.
Certificate reminder

Think of HeyGen videos as micro-presentations—short, intentional, and contextual. They’re not substitutes for full lectures, but they’re great for tone-setting, motivation, and crisp explanations.

5
Ethics, likeness, and disclosure

Because HeyGen uses human-like avatars, ethics matter even more than with text or audio.

  • Do not impersonate real people. Even with permission, be thoughtful about how their likeness is used.
  • Disclose synthetic video when clarity matters, especially in educational and professional settings.
  • Use diverse avatars responsibly. Ensure cultural, racial, and gender representation is respectful and appropriate to the context.
  • Accessibility: Provide captions and transcripts for all generated videos.
Design principle

AI video should clarify communication, not obscure it. If an avatar distracts from the message, simplify the design or switch to audio or text.

Wrap-up: Where HeyGen fits in your AI toolkit

HeyGen is strongest when you want:

  • A short video with a consistent “presenter”
  • Announcements, module intros, tips, or micro-lessons
  • Multi-language versions of the same script
  • A way to build quick visual engagement without recording yourself on camera

The free version encourages intentionality: What message is short and important enough to justify a 30–45 second avatar video?
