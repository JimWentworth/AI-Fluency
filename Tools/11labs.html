Tool Highlight: ElevenLabs (Free Browser Version)

Browser-based AI voices for short clips, intros, and learning audio.

In this walkthrough, you’ll go from creating a free ElevenLabs account to using the browser Text to Speech tools—while staying within your monthly free characters so you don’t run out of audio halfway through a project.

What ElevenLabs is (and what the free tier gives you)

ElevenLabs is a browser-based AI audio platform known for very natural-sounding text to speech, plus tools for dubbing, music, and more.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

For this certificate, we focus on the free plan, which currently includes:​:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  • About 10,000 characters per month of generation on the free tier.
  • Text to Speech in 30+ languages from the web app.
  • Access to a Voice Library of prebuilt voices.
  • Limited access to Studio/Projects, Dubbing, and other creative tools.
Important caveats

On the free plan, you have:

  • A finite number of characters (“credits”) per month.
  • A per-generation character limit (about 2,500 characters per request in the web TTS on free plans).:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • More restrictions on commercial use, voice cloning, and music downloads than paid plans.:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Account choice

Treat this as a personal creative account unless your institution provides a specific ElevenLabs license. Avoid uploading confidential campus documents, sensitive recordings, or regulated data.

Tour the browser interface

  • Top navigation: Tabs for Creative Platform, Agents, Developers, and Pricing.

Mental model

For this program, think of ElevenLabs mainly as: “Type or paste short text → choose a voice → generate a clean audio clip.” The fancy extras (dubbing, long-form studio projects, music) are nice, but not required to practice responsible AI use.

3
Start with Text to Speech (TTS)

Text to Speech is the core workflow: you supply text, pick a voice, and get back audio.:contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

  1. From the homepage, click Text to Speech.
  2. In the text box, paste a short script (aim for 2–6 sentences), such as: “Welcome to our AI Fluency module. In this lesson, you’ll explore how to use generative AI tools critically and responsibly in your coursework.”
  3. In the Voice area:
    • Choose a voice from the default list (or Voice Library).
    • Optionally switch language or accent to match your content and audience.:contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  4. Adjust any available settings (stability, clarity, style) if shown, then click Generate.
  5. Listen to the clip. If it’s useful, download it directly from the TTS page.:contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
Activity: Three versions of the same message

Take a 1–2 sentence announcement for a course. Generate:

  • One formal voice
  • One casual / conversational voice
  • One slower, more “podcast-like” delivery

Compare: which would you actually use with your students or colleagues? Why?

4
Manage free-tier credits and character limits

On the free plan, the main constraint is characters (which function like credits). You get roughly 10,000 characters per month, and each generation consumes part of that allowance.:contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

  • The web TTS UI also has a per-generation character cap (around 2,500 characters for free plans). If you paste more than that, it will refuse or ask you to cut it down.:contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Long scripts (multi-page readings) can eat through your monthly free characters quickly.
  • If you hit your monthly limit, you’ll need to wait until it resets or upgrade to a paid plan to keep generating.:contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
Strategies to avoid running out of characters

To stretch the free tier and avoid “I’m out of tokens” moments:

  • Record only the essentials. Use audio for key intros, summaries, or examples, not every word of a long document.
  • Script short segments. Break longer content into 30–60 second chunks and only generate what you’ll actually use.
  • Reuse scripts. If you find a script you like, save it outside ElevenLabs and regenerate later instead of rewriting long text in the box every time.
  • Check character counts. Many text editors can show character counts before you paste into ElevenLabs.
Certificate reminder

For this program, treat the free ElevenLabs plan as a practice lab for generating a handful of audio artifacts, not as a full audiobook or large-scale podcast production platform.

5
Choose and design voices (within free-tier constraints)

ElevenLabs gives you two main paths to a voice: Voice Library (prebuilt voices) and Voice Design (generate a voice from a text description).:contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

  1. From the navigation, go to Voices (or similar) and explore the Voice Library.
  2. Sample a few voices that fit an academic setting (clear, neutral, appropriate tone).
  3. Optionally, try Voice Design:
    • Describe the voice (age, tone, accent, style).
    • Use a varied preview script so you can hear the full range.:contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
  4. Save 1–2 “house voices” you’ll reuse, instead of constantly switching (this is easier for learners to recognize, and it reduces trial-and-error generations).
Free plan limitations

The free tier restricts certain voice cloning and commercial usage. It’s fine for experiments and non-commercial educational content, but check ElevenLabs’ current terms if you plan to use generated voices in public-facing or monetized projects.:contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

6
A simple workflow: from script to course audio

Here’s a practical way to use ElevenLabs in a course or workshop without burning through your free characters.

  1. Draft a short script (100–300 words) in Docs, Word, or another editor:
    • Module intro,
    • Activity instructions, or
    • A short summary of key ideas.
  2. Check the character count and trim if needed.
  3. Paste into ElevenLabs Text to Speech and generate with your chosen voice.
  4. Download the audio and upload it into your LMS or slide deck.
  5. Keep the script in your own files so you can regenerate or update it later without rewriting from scratch.
Activity: One-minute micro-lecture

Write a ~150-word script explaining one key concept from this certificate. Generate:

  • Version A: straight, neutral reading
  • Version B: slightly more expressive, storytelling style

Ask: which would help a busy student grasp the idea faster? Where might either version confuse or distract them?

7
Ethics, disclosure, and accessibility

ElevenLabs voices are very realistic. That’s powerful—and also a reason to be careful.

  • Disclosure: Consider telling your audience when a voice is synthetic, especially in academic contexts or when the voice could be mistaken for a specific human speaker.
  • No impersonation: Don’t design voices meant to mimic identifiable individuals (colleagues, public figures) without explicit consent.
  • Accessibility: Generated audio is not a replacement for accessible text. Provide transcripts or text alternatives for all key audio.
  • Editing for clarity: If the TTS output mispronounces names or technical terms, adjust your script spelling or provide phonetic hints to steer the pronunciation.
Certificate lens

As you experiment with ElevenLabs, focus on how AI voices change the experience of a message, and where you—as a human instructor, staff member, or student—still need to provide context, empathy, and judgment.

8
Wrap-up: Where ElevenLabs fits in your AI toolkit

ElevenLabs is strongest when you need:

  • Short, polished audio clips from carefully written scripts
  • Experimentation with different voices and styles
  • Quick audio for slides, LMS announcements, and explainer snippets

The free tier’s character limits are a useful constraint: they push you to be intentional about what truly needs an audio version. As you move through the rest of this program, keep asking: “What does audio add here—and what decisions do I still need to make as a human communicator?”

This page focuses on the free browser-based ElevenLabs experience at elevenlabs.io. Plans, limits, and features change over time, but the core workflow—draft, generate, listen, and revise with care—remains the same.
