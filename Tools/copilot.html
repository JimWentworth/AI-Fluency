Tool Highlight: Microsoft Copilot (Campus Data-Protected Version)

A secure, enterprise-grade AI assistant integrated into the Microsoft 365 ecosystem.

In this walkthrough, you’ll move from logging in with Illinois single sign-on, to using Copilot inside the browser, and finally to leveraging the deeper integrations in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams.

Before you start: Use the campus-protected version

Microsoft offers several versions of Copilot. The one you want for university work is the Microsoft 365 / Enterprise Copilot that uses Illinois Entra ID (formerly Azure AD) authentication. This version provides:

  • Commercial data protection (your prompts and data are not used for training)
  • Enterprise-grade privacy aligned with FERPA- and HIPAA-aware workflows
  • Access to your Illinois OneDrive, SharePoint, and Outlook files (with permissions respected)
  • Integration directly into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Teams
Avoid this

Do not use the public copilot.microsoft.com with personal accounts for university-related data. You will not have enterprise protections there. Always check that you're logged in with your @illinois.edu account and see a badge like “Your data is protected.”

1
Log in to Copilot with Illinois SSO
  1. Open https://copilot.microsoft.com or https://www.microsoft365.com.
  2. Click Sign in.
  3. Enter your NetID@illinois.edu email.
  4. You will be redirected to the Illinois login page; authenticate with your NetID and password.
  5. After login, confirm that you see:
    • Your Illinois account avatar in the top right
    • A “Your data is protected” tag or lock icon in the sidebar/banner
If something looks wrong

If Copilot warns that your data might be visible to Microsoft or that data isn't protected, sign out and back in with your **@illinois.edu** identity. This is the most common source of accidental misconfiguration.

2
Choose where to use Copilot

Copilot comes in multiple “surfaces,” each with different strengths. The protected Illinois version works across:

Copilot Web App General

The browser-based chat interface for brainstorming, drafting, summarizing, coding, troubleshooting, and quick “copilot”-style work.

Word + Copilot Writing

Generate outlines, revise drafts, translate tone, and summarize long documents—grounded in the files you open from OneDrive or SharePoint.

Excel + Copilot Analysis

Ask natural-language questions about spreadsheets, generate formulas, analyze patterns, or create visualizations from your existing data.

PowerPoint + Copilot Slides

Create slide decks from documents, summarize long reports visually, redesign layouts, and tweak tone or emphasis.

Outlook + Copilot Email

Summarize long threads, draft responses, and extract tasks—while respecting privacy and permissions from your actual mailbox.

Teams + Copilot Meetings

Summaries, action items, and insights from meetings you attended or missed, with permissions carefully enforced.

Mental model

Copilot behaves differently depending on where you launch it. Using Copilot *inside* Word or OneDrive is not the same as chatting in the browser—it has access to the specific file you are working on.

3
Start in the Copilot Web App (recommended orientation)

To get familiar with Copilot’s reasoning style and its guardrails, start in the web chat experience before using the deeper Microsoft 365 integrations.

  1. Open https://copilot.microsoft.com.
  2. Ask a warm-up question such as:
    “Explain this concept in simple terms: retrieval-augmented generation.”
  3. Then push for critical nuance:
    “Give me limitations or edge cases where this approach fails.”
  4. Upload a file (Word, PDF, or text) and ask:
    • “What is the author’s main argument in this document?”
    • “Create 3 interpretations someone might reasonably challenge.”
Critical-use habit

Copilot is confident but not omniscient. Look for simplifications, incorrect causal claims, or invented details. Treat it as a collaborator, not an authority.

4
Use Copilot inside Word for grounded writing help

Word + Copilot is one of the most powerful combinations for academic and administrative work. Copilot sees the document you’re editing and responds with grounded suggestions.

  1. Open a document stored in OneDrive or SharePoint.
  2. Click the Copilot icon in the right sidebar.
  3. Try prompts such as:
    • “Give me a concise 150-word summary of this document.”
    • “Suggest 3 alternative structures for clearer flow.”
    • “Rewrite this paragraph with more direct language.”
    • “Identify missing counter-arguments or risks.”
  4. Always review changes in Track Changes before accepting them.
Activity: Revision Ladder

Choose one paragraph. Ask Copilot to:

  1. Rewrite it for clarity
  2. Tighten it by 30%
  3. Highlight assumptions the text makes
  4. Draft a counter-argument you should take seriously

Compare the outputs: Where is Copilot helpful? Where does it overreach?

5
Use Copilot in Excel for analysis and visualization

Excel + Copilot excels (sorry) at explaining your data, generating formulas, and helping you reason about patterns—but it cannot validate data quality or contextual correctness.

  1. Open a spreadsheet in OneDrive/SharePoint.
  2. Click Copilot in the ribbon.
  3. Ask:
    • “What trends stand out across these rows?”
    • “Write a formula that calculates X grouped by Y.”
    • “Suggest 3 charts that could summarize this data.”
  4. Expect Copilot to be wrong if your column names are messy—clean them first.
Always apply human judgment

Copilot does not know your data’s meaning. A beautiful chart is not the same as a correct one. Verify every claim against the raw data.

6
Use Copilot in PowerPoint for slide generation

This is where Copilot frequently surprises people—it can turn a Word document into a first-draft slide deck in seconds.

  1. Open PowerPoint and click Copilot.
  2. Select Create presentation from file.
  3. Choose a document from your OneDrive.
  4. After generation, try:
    • “Reduce the slide count by 40% but keep key arguments.”
    • “Add speaker notes summarizing evidence from the source document.”
Activity: Slide Critique

Compare Copilot’s deck to the source text. Identify:

  • Where nuance was lost
  • Where visuals mislead or distort emphasis
  • Which slides are strong enough to reuse
7
Use Copilot in Outlook and Teams

Outlook

  • Summarize long threads
  • Suggest responses in your voice
  • Extract action items and deadlines

Teams

  • Generate meeting summaries (only if you were invited)
  • Highlight decisions, action items, and risks
  • Provide follow-up questions or next steps
Permissions matter

Copilot can only summarize content you had access to. If you weren’t invited to a meeting or shared on a document, Copilot won’t see it.

8
Wrap-up: Fit within AI Fluency

Copilot is best viewed as a workflow amplifier rather than a research-analysis engine. Its strengths come from:

  • Deep integration with your files and communication tools
  • Ability to draft, refine, and reorganize work quickly
  • Support for administrative and instructional tasks

As you continue through this program, ask: When is Copilot helping me move faster, and when is it tempting me to skip the reasoning step I actually need to do myself?

This page reflects capabilities of the enterprise-protected Copilot available to the UIUC community. Microsoft updates Copilot frequently, so icons and menu names may shift—but the underlying workflows remain stable.
